The Sun • सूर्य

The star at the centre of our Solar System | हमारे सौर मंडल के केंद्र में स्थित तारा

⭐ It is a STAR, not a planet 📏 1.39 million km wide 🌡️ Surface: ~5,500°C 🌍 Distance from Earth: ~150 million km 💡 All energy on Earth comes from the Sun!

The 8 Planets • 8 ग्रह

सूर्य से दूरी के क्रम में (nearest to farthest from the Sun)

☿
1. Mercury
बुध

Mercury is the smallest planet and closest to the Sun. It has no atmosphere, so temperatures go from very hot (+430°C) to very cold (-180°C)!

📏 Size: 4,880 km diameter | ☀️ Distance from Sun: 57.9 million km
🌙 Moons: 0 | 🔄 One year = 88 Earth days
♀️
2. Venus
शुक्र

Venus is the hottest planet (not Mercury!) because of its thick atmosphere. It is also the brightest object in the night sky after the Moon. It spins in the opposite direction!

📏 Size: 12,104 km | ☀️ 108 million km from Sun
🌙 Moons: 0 | 🔄 One year = 225 Earth days
🌍
3. Earth
पृथ्वी

Our home! Earth is the only known planet with life. It has liquid water, a protective atmosphere and one Moon. 71% of Earth is covered by water!

📏 Size: 12,742 km | ☀️ 150 million km from Sun
🌙 Moons: 1 | 🔄 One year = 365.25 days
🔴
4. Mars
मंगल

Mars is called the "Red Planet" because of its reddish iron oxide surface. It has the tallest volcano in the Solar System — Olympus Mons (27 km high!). Scientists are trying to send humans to Mars!

📏 Size: 6,779 km | ☀️ 228 million km from Sun
🌙 Moons: 2 (Phobos & Deimos) | 🔄 One year = 687 Earth days
🪐
5. Jupiter
बृहस्पति

Jupiter is the LARGEST planet — 1,300 Earths could fit inside it! It has a giant storm called the "Great Red Spot" that has been raging for over 400 years. It is a gas giant — no solid surface!

📏 Size: 139,820 km | ☀️ 779 million km from Sun
🌙 Moons: 95 | 🔄 One year = 12 Earth years
🪐
6. Saturn
शनि

Saturn is famous for its beautiful RINGS made of ice and rock! It is the least dense planet — it could float in water! It has 146 known moons, including Titan which has its own atmosphere.

📏 Size: 116,460 km | ☀️ 1.43 billion km from Sun
🌙 Moons: 146 | 🔄 One year = 29 Earth years
🔵
7. Uranus
अरुण

Uranus is an ice giant with a blue-green colour. It is unique because it rotates on its SIDE — tilted at 98°! It is the coldest planet in the Solar System (-224°C).

📏 Size: 50,724 km | ☀️ 2.87 billion km from Sun
🌙 Moons: 28 | 🔄 One year = 84 Earth years
🌀
8. Neptune
वरुण

Neptune is the farthest planet from the Sun. It has the strongest winds in the Solar System — up to 2,100 km/h! It takes 165 Earth years to orbit the Sun once. It was discovered in 1846.

📏 Size: 49,244 km | ☀️ 4.5 billion km from Sun
🌙 Moons: 16 | 🔄 One year = 165 Earth years
💡 How to Remember Planet Order! • ग्रहों का क्रम याद रखें!
